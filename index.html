Programme Overview

The M.A. in Legislative Drafting explores the connection between lawmaking, governance, and public policy. Through an interdisciplinary approach, the programme examines how legislation structures institutions, defines rights and obligations, and shapes social and economic systems.

It also helps learners understand how legislative frameworks guide governance, the role of ministries and legislatures in drafting laws, and the challenges created by complex legal language—highlighting the importance of clear, accessible, and well-structured legislation.

Pathways

Graduates gain the expertise to contribute effectively to legislative and policy development within governance and regulatory systems.

Draft Primary Legislation

Prepare Bills and Acts with clear structure, legal precision, and enforceable provisions.

Ensure Constitutional Compliance

Assess legislative competence, fundamental rights compatibility, and federal balance while drafting laws.

Apply Statutory Interpretation

Design legislation that reduces ambiguity and minimises potential litigation.

Produce Citizen-Centric Legislation

Develop plain-language legal text that improves accessibility without compromising legal accuracy.

Draft Subordinate Legislation

Prepare rules, regulations, and delegated instruments within established statutory authority.
